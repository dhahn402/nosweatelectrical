Stay safe

Warning signs it's time to call an electrician

Most electrical fires start small and silent. If you notice any of these, don't wait — get it looked at.

🔥

Warm or discolored outlets

An outlet or switch that's hot to the touch, buzzing, or browned around the edges is a fire waiting to happen. Stop using it and call.

💡

Flickering or dimming lights

Lights that flicker when the AC or microwave kicks on can mean an overloaded circuit or a loose, arcing connection.

👃

A burning or fishy smell

That faint "hot plastic" or fishy odor near outlets or the panel is overheating wiring. Kill the breaker and call right away.

⚡

Breakers that keep tripping

A breaker's job is to trip — but if it's constant, the circuit is overloaded or there's a fault. It's protecting you; listen to it.

🧯

An old fuse box or aluminum wiring

Fuse panels, aluminum branch wiring, and ungrounded two-prong outlets are known fire risks in older homes. Worth an inspection.

🚿

No GFCI near water

Kitchens, baths, garages, and outdoor outlets need GFCI protection. If yours don't have it, that's a shock and code issue.

New Bern know-how

Local rules & coastal-smart wiring

A few honest things every New Bern & Craven County homeowner should know.

✓

Permits protect you

Most electrical work in New Bern & Craven County needs a permit and inspection. It's not red tape — it's what keeps your insurance valid and your home safe. A licensed electrician handles it for you.

✓

Licensed work matters

In NC, electrical contracting requires a state license. Unlicensed work can void your insurance and your home sale. Always confirm your electrician is NC-licensed and insured (ours is).

✓

Storm & generator safety

Living on the coast means outages. A standby generator needs a proper transfer switch — never backfeed a generator into a wall outlet; it can kill a lineworker and burn your house. We wire it the safe way.

✓

Surge & flood smarts

Whole-home surge protection guards your electronics from storm spikes, and in flood-prone spots we can raise outlets and panels above the line. Coastal homes need coastal wiring.

Helpful info, honestly given — not a substitute for an in-person inspection. When in doubt, it's always cheaper (and safer) to ask than to guess.
